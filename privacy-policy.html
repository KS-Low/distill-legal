Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 12, 2026

Distill is a local media deduplication app. Distill operates entirely on your device and never transmits any personal data over the internet. Distill has no INTERNET permission and is technically incapable of making network connections.

What Distill Processes Locally

To find duplicate photos and videos, Distill:

  • Accesses media files in folders you select via Android's Storage Access Framework (SAF)
  • Computes perceptual fingerprints (hashes) of your media files entirely on-device
  • Stores fingerprints and file metadata (filename, size, date modified) in a local database on your device to improve scan performance

None of this data ever leaves your device.

What Distill Does Not Do

  • Collect analytics or usage statistics
  • Send crash reports to remote servers
  • Display advertisements
  • Use third-party tracking or analytics SDKs
  • Access your location, contacts, microphone, or camera
  • Upload your photos or videos
  • Require or use an account or login

Deletion & Trash

When you delete files with Distill, they are first moved to a trash folder (.distill-trash) inside the folder you selected — nothing is permanently deleted right away. Trashed files are kept for 30 days and then permanently deleted automatically; before that you can restore them or delete them permanently yourself at any time. Because the trash lives inside your own folder, clearing the app's data or uninstalling the app does not remove those files; if the deletion record is lost this way, the affected files are kept indefinitely instead of being auto-deleted.

Local Data Storage

Data Distill generates for itself (fingerprint cache, preferences) is stored in the app's private storage on your device; you can clear it at any time via Android Settings → Apps → Distill → Storage → Clear Data. The one exception is the trash folder described above: it lives inside the folder you selected, so clearing app data does not touch it — you can remove it with any file manager.

Purchases

Distill offers an optional one-time Pro upgrade sold through Google Play's in-app billing. Payment is handled entirely by Google Play under Google's own terms and privacy policy — Distill never sees or stores your payment details. The app only checks whether the upgrade has been purchased, on-device, through Google Play services; Distill itself transmits nothing.

Your Rights (Singapore PDPA)

Distill is developed by an individual based in Singapore and operated in accordance with Singapore's Personal Data Protection Act 2012 (PDPA). Since Distill does not collect or transmit personal data off your device, the standard rights of access, correction, and deletion are exercised entirely through your device's own storage controls.
